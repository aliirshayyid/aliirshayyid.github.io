<span class='anchor' id='about-me'></span>

I am a <span class="accent-text">Postdoctoral Researcher</span> in the **Electrified and Intelligent Systems Automation (EISA) Lab** at **Oakland University**, where I work with <span class="primary-gradient-text">Prof. Jun Chen</span>. I received my **Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering** from Oakland University in December 2024 (GPA 3.96/4.0), and my **B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering** from Wasit University, Iraq.

My research sits at the intersection of **reinforcement learning**, **optimal control**, and **intelligent transportation & energy systems**. During my Ph.D., I developed single- and multi-agent reinforcement learning methods for the **cooperative merging control of connected and autonomous vehicles**, validated in high-fidelity traffic simulators such as SUMO. My current work focuses on **reinforcement learning–based battery management systems** for reconfigurable battery packs. I have (co-)authored more than 15 papers in venues including *IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Vehicles*, *IEEE Transactions on Transportation Electrification*, *Sensors*, *ACC*, and *CCTA*.

I previously served as the **Chair of the IEEE Student Branch** at Oakland University and as **President of the Smart Vehicle Club**, leading a student team to build an autonomous vehicle for the Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition (IGVC).

Feel free to reach out at **[aliirshayyid@oakland.edu](mailto:aliirshayyid@oakland.edu)** if you'd like to discuss research or potential collaboration!
